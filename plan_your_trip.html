Plan your trip!

How to do it

The most economical way to go on a holiday is to book tickets for plane before, at least, 3 or 4 months. Theoretically, the tickets for all routes should be much cheaper unless you don't buy them in the last second.

If you are travelling oftenly with planes, there are some plane companies that make reductions.

Planning a long trip can be a daunting task. Where do you begin? What's step one? What's step two? What's step three? It's easy to get overwhelmed in the beginning, especially when you haven't done something like this before.

How long you need

This step is really easy. Just take your time so that, after the holiday ends, you will be relaxed. It is up to you.

When to go

It depends what type of holiday is. If it is a skiing holiday the best season is in the winter, although I admit that ,there are some places, for example Chile, where there is always snow on the mountains.

You have to do a small research for every place where you want to go because every destination must be seen in a propitious time. Paris must be seen in summer because you need to walk very much on boulevards.Japan must be seen in spring because it is the time when the cherry trees bloom.

Where to go

This is not really a big problem. Go somewhere you will feel relaxed, somewhere you can do all the things you like, somewhere different than your current destination.

Amsterdam: Amsterdam is the capital city and most populous city of the Kingdom of the Netherlands.

Eindhoven: Eindhoven is a municipality and a city located in the province of North Brabant in the south of the Netherlands, originally at the confluence of the Dommel and Gender streams.

Groningen: Groningen is the main municipality as well as the capital city of the eponymous province in the Netherlands.

Zakhintos: Zakynthos is a Greek island in the Ionian Sea. It is the third largest of the Ionian Islands.

Lefkada: Lefkada, or Leucas or Leucadia or Lefkas or Leukas is a Greek island in the Ionian Sea on the west coast of Greece, connected to the mainland by a long causeway and floating bridge.
